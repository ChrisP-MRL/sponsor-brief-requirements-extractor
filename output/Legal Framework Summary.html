Executive Summary

UAV operations for maritime draft surveys in Australian ports require dual compliance: federal aviation regulations administered by CASA (Civil Aviation Safety Authority) and port-specific operational procedures from the relevant port authority. The Legal Framework folder contains foundational CASA regulations and comparable port procedures, scoring 28-90/100 for relevance to Onslow operations.

● Key Finding

High-relevance documents (72-90/100) establish mandatory operational constraints—Visual Line of Sight (VLOS), 400ft altitude limits, 30m separation distances, and operator licensing requirements. However, critical gaps exist: no Onslow-specific port procedures, missing Part 101 Manual of Standards, and unclear CASA approval pathways for operations over "populous areas" (ports).

Compliance Path

Operators must obtain:

  1. CASA RPA accreditation/Remote Pilot License
  2. Aircraft registration
  3. CASA approval for port operations
  4. Pilbara Ports Authority operational permit with 72-hour advance notification to harbor master and VTS

◆ High Relevance (71-100/100)

1. CASA Advisory Circular AC 101-01 Annex B NEW

File: annex-b-advisory-circular-101-01-applying-approval-conduct-many-rpa-operations.pdf
Relevance Score: 95/100

Summary: CASA official guidance on ReOC approval process for one-to-many/swarm RPA operations including application requirements (CONOPS, operational procedures, risk assessments), assessment process (desktop review plus on-site inspection), 3-month processing timeline, fee structure, and operational area specifications.

Why This Is Critical: This document is the critical missing piece addressing the approval pathway gap. While oriented toward one-to-many operations, it reveals CASA's complete approval process framework applicable to ANY non-standard RPA operations requiring Regulation 101.029 approval.

Key Contributions:
  • Application Process: Submit RPAS Multi-purpose Application Form minimum 3 months before intended operations
  • Mandatory Documentation: CONOPS with coordinates, operational procedures, emergency procedures, stakeholder engagement plan, RPA technical manual, landowner/water authority permission
  • Assessment Phases: Desktop assessment → Training/testing approval → On-site technical assessment → Final review
  • Risk Management: Containment requirements, situational awareness protocols, stakeholder coordination, abnormal operations procedures
  • Operational Area Options: Location-specific (e.g., "Onslow Port only") or location-agnostic (Australia-wide)

Transformative Value: Changes "unclear CASA approval pathway" from critical gap to actionable roadmap with clear timeline (minimum 3 months), documentation requirements, and assessment criteria.

2. CASR Part 101 Plain English Guide

File: part-101-micro-excluded-rpa-operations-plain-english-guide-interactive.pdf
Relevance Score: 90/100

Summary: Federal CASA regulation establishing foundational RPA operational requirements: VLOS mandate, 120m altitude limit, 30m separation from persons, daylight-only operations, operator accreditation, and aircraft registration. Defines "populous area" restrictions directly applicable to port operations.

Why This Matters: This is the authoritative federal regulation governing ALL small RPA operations in Australia. It establishes non-negotiable requirements that form the foundation for all maritime drone operations.

Core Requirements:
  • Visual Line of Sight (cannot use FPV as substitute)
  • 120m/400ft maximum altitude
  • 30m minimum lateral separation from non-associated persons
  • Prohibition on flying over people
  • Daylight operations only
  • Ports classified as likely "populous areas" requiring special CASA approval

3. Drone Operations Over Port Lands and Waters Procedure ENHANCED

File: Drone_Operations_Over_Port_Lands_and_Waters_Procedure__A1038610_.pdf (Mid West Ports Authority)
Relevance Score: 85/100

Summary: Mid West Ports Authority procedure for Geraldton Port establishing permit-to-work system with multi-level approval chain, mandatory JSEA risk assessment, 60m marine mammal separation, 100m Osprey nest exclusion zone, MSIC security card requirements, and VTS/harbor master vessel notification protocols.

Why This Is Valuable: Provides a comprehensive, proven operational framework specifically designed for commercial port drone operations in Western Australia. Demonstrates how federal CASA Part 101 regulations are implemented at the port authority level.

Key Provisions:
  • Permit Application Process: Multi-tier approval workflow (Harbour Master → Duty Ops → Port Security → CEO)
  • Operator Qualifications: RePL/ReOC/RPA required; operators of >2kg drones without RePL or ReOC will NOT be authorized

  • Documentation: Flight path plan, JSEA/risk assessment, pilot license, drone registration

Transferability to Onslow: As both Geraldton (MWPA) and Onslow (Pilbara Ports Authority) operate under the same state legislation, this procedural framework is highly transferable despite being Geraldton-specific (15-point deduction).

Critical Regulatory Requirements for Onslow OGV Draft Survey Operations

Federal Requirements (CASA)

Operator Qualifications

  • Small/Medium RPA (>250g): Remote Pilot License (RePL) OR Remotely Piloted Aircraft Operator Certificate (ReOC) mandatory
  • Age: Minimum 16 years for accreditation; under-16 operators require adult (18+) supervision
  • Aviation Reference Number (ARN): Required to access myCASA portal for licensing/registration

Aircraft Requirements

  • Registration: RPA must be registered with CASA before first commercial flight
  • Maintenance: Must be maintained in airworthy condition; pre-flight checks mandatory
  • Markings: Nationality and registration marks displayed per Part 45 specifications

Operational Restrictions

  • Visual Line of Sight (VLOS): Mandatory at all times; electronic aids (FPV, screens) cannot replace VLOS
  • Altitude: Maximum 120m (400ft) AGL unless CASA approval obtained
  • Separation Distances:
    • 30m minimum lateral distance from non-associated persons
    • Prohibition on flying over people regardless of altitude
  • Weather: Operations only in good visibility; prohibited in cloud, fog, or low visibility
  • Daylight Only: Night operations prohibited for excluded RPA
  • Populous Areas: CRITICAL - Ports likely classified as populous; CASA approval required

Record Keeping

  • Operational logs: nature, location, altitude, dates, times, controller ARN, serviceability records
  • Incident reporting: Near-misses, equipment failures, safety concerns

Port Authority Requirements (Based on MWPA Model)

Permit and Approval

  • Application for Drone Operations Permit required
  • Approval chain: Harbour Master → Duty Operations Supervisor → Port Security Officer → CEO (if media involved)
  • 72-hour advance notification (based on Ashburton dive operations model)

Risk Assessment

JSEA (Job Safety and Environmental Analysis) mandatory before operations, including:

  • Flight path and waypoints
  • Landing/takeoff areas
  • Flight times and duration
  • Flight height profile
  • Purpose and operational details
  • Hazard identification and mitigation

Documentation Required

  • Copy of pilot license/accreditation (RePL/ReOC)
  • Aircraft registration certificate
  • Flight path plan with coordinates
  • Public liability insurance (recommended)

Maritime-Specific Restrictions

  • Marine Mammals: 60m minimum separation from whales, dolphins, sea lions
  • Wildlife: 100m exclusion zones around nesting sites (if applicable)
  • Vessel Operations: Must not pose hazard or distraction to shipping activities
  • Security Zones: Maritime Security Identification Card (MSIC) may be required if operating within Port Security Zone

Coordination Protocols

  • Harbor Master notification: Must notify before operations
  • VTS (Vessel Traffic Service): Integration with port traffic monitoring
  • Vessel Operators: Harbor master notifies ship agents; vessel masters must be aware of drone activities
  • No interference with pilotage or shipping movements

Environmental Considerations

  • Noise minimization required
  • No dropping/discharging objects unless no hazard to aircraft, people, or property
  • Weather conditions: Wind, temperature, moisture limits per manufacturer specifications
  • Visibility over water: Particularly challenging due to glare and environmental conditions

Recommended Next Steps

Immediate Actions (Before Operations)

1. Obtain Onslow-Specific Documentation

  • Contact Pilbara Ports Authority to request:
    • Drone operations procedure for Onslow Port (if exists)
    • Onslow Port Handbook with operational boundaries, VTS procedures, security zones
    • Port airspace restrictions and coordination protocols
  • Verify Onslow airspace classification via CASA airspace charts:
    • Check for controlled aerodrome restrictions (5.5km radius)
    • Identify restricted areas, danger areas, or military operating zones
    • Confirm Class G airspace designation

2. Acquire Missing CASA Documentation and Initiate Approval Process

  • Download Part 101 Manual of Standards from CASA website (casa.gov.au)
  • Prepare CASA Approval Application (Based on AC 101-01 Annex B Guidance):
    • Form: Obtain RPAS Multi-purpose Application Form
    • Lead Time: Submit minimum 3 months before intended operations
    • Prepare CONOPS: Onslow Port area in coordinates, altitude profile, operating dates/times
    • Operational Procedures: Pre-flight checks, VTS coordination, vessel notification
    • Emergency Procedures: Loss of VLOS, GPS failure, airspace incursion protocols
    • Stakeholder Engagement Plan: Pilbara Ports, harbour master, ship agents, emergency services
    • Supporting Documentation: RPA manual, landowner permission, insurance, licenses
  • Contact CASA Regional Office (Perth): Pre-application consultation, clarify maritime-specific criteria

3. Establish Operator Compliance

  • Internal Staff Pathway: Enroll in CASA-accredited training, obtain RePL/ReOC, register RPA, develop procedures
  • Contractor Pathway: Verify RePL/ReOC, confirm insurance coverage (minimum $20M recommended), establish safety protocols

4. Develop Operational Procedures

  • Model on MWPA framework with Onslow adaptations:
    • Permit-to-work application form
    • JSEA risk assessment template
    • 72-hour advance notification protocol
    • Marine mammal observation and avoidance procedures
  • Integrate with Onslow Port systems: VTS communication, harbour master coordination, security zone access

Medium-Term Actions (Operational Preparedness)

5. Apply for CASA Approvals

  • Submit application for populous area operations with risk mitigation demonstration
  • Request altitude approval if surveys require >400ft operations

6. Engage Stakeholders

  • Port Authority: Present operational concept, establish permit process
  • Vessel Operators: Coordinate with ship agents, establish communication protocols
  • CASA Regional Office: Brief on operational concept, seek compliance guidance

7. Conduct Trial Operations

  • Desktop simulation: Flight path planning, JSEA risk assessment, emergency procedure rehearsal
  • Controlled trial: Initial survey with full safety protocols, Port Authority/CASA observer presence
  • Operational validation: Verify sensor accuracy, confirm compliance, validate communication protocols

Long-Term Actions (Continuous Improvement)

8. Establish Safety Management System

  • Periodic risk reviews (quarterly or after incidents)
  • Annual refresher training for operators
  • Regular RPA maintenance and pre-flight checks
  • Incident analysis with root cause investigation

9. Regulatory Monitoring

  • Monitor CASA updates to Part 101 and Manual of Standards
  • Stay current with Pilbara Ports Authority operational requirements
  • Track Australian drone industry best practices

10. Expand Operational Capability

  • BVLOS: If surveys exceed VLOS range, pursue Beyond Visual Line of Sight approval
  • Night operations: If required for efficiency, seek approval and implement lighting systems
  • Autonomous operations: Explore automated survey patterns as regulations evolve
