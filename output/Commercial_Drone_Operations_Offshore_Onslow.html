2. Applicable Regulatory Framework for >2kg Drones

2.1 Remote Pilot Licence (RePL) Requirement

Mandatory Requirement

If your drone weighs more than 2 kg, you cannot operate it commercially under CASA's excluded category. You must obtain a Remote Pilot Licence (RePL).[5]

"An RePL is required to fly a drone larger than 2kg for commercial operations, and outside drone safety rules."[6]

This is a non-negotiable requirement regardless of whether your operations are offshore or onshore.

2.2 Remote Operator's Certificate (ReOC)

In addition to the individual pilot's RePL, for commercial operations you'll generally need a Remote Pilot Licence (RePL) and an Operator's Certificate (OC).[7]

The Operator's Certificate represents organizational-level compliance and insurance considerations.

3. Airspace Classification Considerations

3.1 Offshore Class G Airspace

While specific offshore classifications aren't detailed in the documentation, the general principle is that most RPAS/drone operations are conducted in Class G (General/Uncontrolled) airspace.[8] This would reasonably extend to offshore areas beyond controlled aerodrome influence zones.

Class G Airspace Implications

In Class G airspace, no ATC services are provided to VFR or IFR traffic.[9] This means:

  • Full responsibility: You bear full responsibility for detecting and avoiding other aircraft[10]
  • No separation services: No separation services are provided by air traffic control[11]
  • Situational awareness: Situational awareness becomes entirely your responsibility[12]

3.2 Distance from Controlled Aerodromes

4. Standard Operating Conditions for >2kg Offshore Operations

4.1 Visual Line of Sight (VLOS) Requirement

"Core operating rules include flying below 120 m AGL, keeping visual line of sight, maintaining 30 m separation from people and staying out of controlled airspace without approval."[13]

⚠ Critical Challenge for Offshore Operations

The visual line of sight (VLOS) requirement becomes particularly challenging in an offshore environment at 20 km distance. You must maintain continuous unaided visual contact with your drone at all times.[14]

Operating at 20 km offshore likely presents practical and legal challenges to maintaining VLOS, unless you are operating from a marine vessel in that location.

4.2 Altitude Restrictions

Maximum Altitude: 120 Meter (393.7 Feet) in uncontrolled airspace.[15]

This altitude ceiling applies equally to offshore operations in Class G airspace.

4.3 People Separation Requirements

You must stay below 120 metres and keep more than 30 metres away from other people.[16]

In an offshore environment 20 km from land, this requirement becomes context-dependent—you must maintain 30 m separation from any personnel on your vessel or other vessels in the operational area.
