1. Introduction: Establishing the Regulatory Foundation

1.1 The Role of Classification Societies

Classification societies serve as the independent organizations responsible for verifying compliance with international maritime regulations. Their approval of any technology or methodology represents the gold standard for maritime industry acceptance.Source: Maritime Drone Manufacturers, p.27

Key Classification Societies:
ABS DNV Bureau Veritas Lloyd's Register RINA ClassNK

1.2 Remote Inspection Techniques (RIT) Framework

Classification societies have formalized drone operations under the Remote Inspection Techniques (RIT) framework, which establishes standardized approaches for:

  • Equipment specification and certification
  • Operator training and qualification
  • Data management and quality assurance protocols
  • Service provider certification schemes

Formal Definition: "Remote Inspection Techniques (RIT) refers to methods used to assess and monitor the condition or performance of equipment, structures, or systems from a distance, without the need for physical presence."Source: Survey Applications, citation 121

RIT includes UAVs (drones), ROVs, climbers, and other remote technologies formally recognized by IACS Recommendation 42.Source: Maritime Manufacturers, citation 43

2. Comprehensive Regulatory Framework Development

2.1 ABS (American Bureau of Shipping) Leadership

ABS released industry-leading Guidance Notes on Using Unmanned Aerial Vehicles, adopting "a two-pronged approach, laying out a certification scheme for UAV inspection service providers and outlining actionable advice on class surveys and non-class inspections."Source: Survey Applications, citation 1

Regulatory Milestone

"This new guidance is an important element in advancing safety and consistency in the application of drone technology," according to Liberty Maritime Corporation Engineering Vice President Theodore S. Makrinos.Source: Survey Applications, citation 1

The guidance framework was developed through extensive marine and offshore trial work, covering equipment specification, data management, associated management systems, recommended standards for service providers, and training.Source: Survey Applications, citation 137

2.2 DNV GL Framework

DNV GL established formal approval processes for remote inspection service providers as early as January 2019, approving "the use of service suppliers to provide close-up surveys using remote RITs—drones, climbers or remotely operated vehicles (ROVs)."

2.3 Bureau Veritas Pioneering Role

Bureau Veritas integrated Remote Inspection Techniques into its classification rules in 2019, establishing a comprehensive certification scheme for RIT service providers. As of the research date, Bureau Veritas had approved 46 RIT service suppliers worldwide to support ship and offshore unit owners with drone use.

2.4 Lloyd's Register and RINA Integration

Both Lloyd's Register and RINA developed parallel approval frameworks, creating a comprehensive global regulatory ecosystem where drone operations are recognized across multiple jurisdictions and classification societies.

8.3 Technical Validation Requirements

Classification Process for Autonomous Vessels

The certification process includes:

  • System-level analysis: Detailed review of autonomous navigation, power, propulsion, and control systems
  • Construction survey: Verification of vessel construction against approved plans
  • Sea trials: Operational validation under real-world conditions
  • Safety requirements validation: Demonstration that USV meets critical maritime safety requirements

Implications: If classification societies can approve fully autonomous ocean-going vessels, the technical and regulatory framework clearly exists to support drone-based draft surveys with appropriate operational protocols.
