## Applicable Regulatory Framework for >2kg Drones

### Remote Pilot Licence (RePL) Requirement

**Your most critical regulatory requirement is straightforward: if your drone weighs more than 2 kg, you cannot operate it commercially under CASA's excluded category. You must obtain a Remote Pilot Licence (RePL)**<citation href="https://www.casa.gov.au/knowyourdrone/drone-rules" title="Drone rules | Civil Aviation Safety Authority">5</citation>.

**An RePL is required to fly a drone larger than 2kg for commercial operations, and outside drone safety rules**<citation href="https://www.flyingglass.com.au/drone-rules-under-250g-australia-legal/" title="Drone Rules Under 250g Australia: Complete 2026 Legal Guide">6</citation>. This is a non-negotiable requirement regardless of whether your operations are offshore or onshore.

### Remote Operator's Certificate (ReOC)

In addition to the individual pilot's RePL, **for commercial operations you'll generally need a Remote Pilot Licence (RePL) and an Operator's Certificate (OC)**<citation href="https://www.propelrc.com/drone-laws-in-australia/" title="Drone Laws in Australia (June 2026): Complete Guide">7</citation>. The Operator's Certificate represents organizational-level compliance and insurance considerations.

## Airspace Classification Considerations

### Offshore Class G Airspace

While specific offshore classifications aren't detailed in the documentation, the general principle is that **most RPAS/drone operations are conducted in Class G (General/Uncontrolled) airspace**<citation href="https://www.flyeye.io/australian-drone-regulations-drone-class/" title="Australian Regulations by Drone Class - Fly Eye">8</citation>. ## Standard Operating Conditions for >2kg Offshore Operations

### Visual Line of Sight (VLOS) Requirement

**Core operating rules include flying below 120 m AGL, keeping visual line of sight, maintaining 30 m separation from people and staying out of controlled airspace without approval**<citation href="https://dronesgator.com/australia-drone-laws/" title="All Drone Laws In Australia In 2026 (Ultimate Guide)">13</citation>.

The **visual line of sight (VLOS)** requirement becomes particularly challenging in an offshore environment at 20 km distance. You must maintain **continuous unaided visual contact with your drone at all times**<citation href="https://ts2.tech/en/australian-drone-laws-2025-complete-guide-to-rules-registration-no-fly-zones/" title="Australian Drone Laws 2025: Complete Guide to Rules, Registration & No-Fly Zones">14</citation>. Operating at 20 km offshore likely presents practical and legal challenges to maintaining VLOS, unless you are operating from a marine vessel in that location.

### Altitude Restrictions

**Maximum Altitude: 120 Meter (393.7 Feet) in uncontrolled airspace**<citation href="https://tolluncrewedsystems.com/drone-licence/australian-drone-licence-guide/" title="Complete Australian Drone Licence Guide 2026 | Toll Uncrewed Systems">15</citation>. This altitude ceiling applies equally to offshore operations in Class G airspace.

### People Separation Requirements

**You must stay below 120 metres and keep more than 30 metres away from other people**<citation href="https://www.spheredrones.com.au/resources/blog/commercial-drone-laws" title="Commercial Drone Laws in Australia: What You Need to Know">16</citation>. In an offshore environment 20 km from land, this requirement becomes context-dependent—you must maintain 30 m separation from any personnel on your vessel or other vessels in the operational area.
